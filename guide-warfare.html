1. The Nexus System

The Nexus is the physical and political heart of every town. It appears as a regular-sized end crystal and serves as the town's war objective. Interacting with a Nexus or using the /nexus command opens the war management GUI, through which wars are declared, armies are managed, and raids are initiated.

Nexus Mechanics

Each Nexus has 1,000 hit points. During a siege (full-scale war), attackers can damage the Nexus. When its HP reaches zero, the town is considered defeated and is dissolved. During peacetime, the Nexus is invulnerable.

Accessibility Requirements

The Nexus must be placed in a location that attackers can reasonably reach. Staff enforce the following guidelines:

  • Maximum depth: Cannot be buried more than 15 blocks underground
  • Maximum height: Cannot be suspended more than 50 blocks above the highest point of any building or the natural ground
  • Example: If you build a castle spanning from Y=50 to Y=85, the Nexus cannot be placed above Y=135 or below Y=35 unless you provide a clearly defined tunnel, bridge, or path that attackers can follow (walls and defenses are still permitted along this path).

Nexus Stats

  • HP: 1,000
  • Appearance: End Crystal
  • Movable: /nexus move
  • Invulnerable: During peace

Placement Rules

  • Max depth: 15 blocks
  • Max height: 50 blocks above highest structure
  • Must have defined path if outside bounds

Nexus Commands

/nexus
Opens the Nexus GUI for managing wars, armies, raids, and viewing status.
/nexus move
Moves your town's Nexus to your current location. Cannot be used during an active war.
/nexus remove
Permanently removes the town's Nexus. Only available during peacetime.
/nexus create
Creates a new Nexus for your town if one does not exist.
Staff Enforcement: Staff will relocate improperly placed Nexuses that violate accessibility rules. Nexus cannot be moved during active wars, so ensure proper placement beforehand.

2. Army System

To wage war, a nation must first organize its fighting force into a structured army. The army system is managed through the Nexus GUI and allows for organized military campaigns and earned special abilities.

Army Structure

The military is organized hierarchically:

  • 3 Army Groups total per nation
  • 3 Regiments per Army Group
  • Each regiment can have its own leadership and earns Regiment Points (RP) through combat

Creating an Army

The nation leader initiates army creation by being the first to join a regiment:

/army join (regiment)
The first use of this command by a nation leader creates the army group and regiment.

After creation, the nation leader must appoint a General for the army group (this can be themselves or another trusted player). The General can then appoint Regiment Leaders for each of the three regiments. While not required, this allows for realistic military hierarchies and delegation of command.

Regiment Points (RP) & Formations

When a member of a regiment kills an enemy player during wartime or raids, their regiment earns Regiment Points (RP). These points are pooled at the regiment level and can be spent by regiment leaders to purchase formations that grant temporary buffs and strategic advantages.

Army Hierarchy

  • 3 Army Groups total
  • 3 Regiments per group
  • General leads army group
  • Regiment leaders optional

Regiment Points

  • Earned from player kills
  • Pooled by regiment
  • Spent on formations
  • Managed via Nexus GUI

Leadership Strategy: Appointing regiment leaders allows for more responsive command during battle, as they can purchase formations for their troops without needing to contact the General. Consider a chain of command that mirrors your nation's activity schedules.

3. Full-Scale Warfare & Sieges

A formal declaration of war initiates a siege—a state of total conflict where block destruction is enabled and the enemy Nexus can be attacked. Unlike raids, sieges have no time limit and end only when one side's Nexus is destroyed or a peace treaty is signed.

Siege Mechanics

  • PvP Enabled: Combat is forced between warring parties
  • Block Destruction Enabled: Attackers can break through defenses
  • Nexus Vulnerable: The enemy Nexus can be damaged (1,000 HP)
  • No Time Limit: Sieges continue until resolution

Prerequisites for Declaring War

  • Diplomatic Status: Your nation must have the target set as an enemy
  • Organized Army: Your nation must have at least one army group formed
  • Peace: Neither party can already be at war with each other
  • Active Nexus: Both sides must have valid, placed Nexuses
  • Permission: You must be a nation leader or have war permissions

War Commands

/war declare nation <nation>
Declares war on an entire nation and all its member towns.
/war declare town <town>
Declares war on a specific town.
/war status
Checks the current status of any wars your nation or town is involved in.
/war list
Lists all active wars currently happening on the server.

Siege Features

  • PvP enabled
  • Block destruction enabled
  • Nexus damageable (1,000 HP)
  • No time limit

Requirements

  • Enemy diplomatic status
  • Organized army
  • Both sides at peace
  • Valid Nexuses

4. Raids (Work in Progress)

Raids are limited, time-bound hostile actions initiated through the Nexus GUI. Unlike sieges, raids do not allow block destruction or Nexus damage. They serve as limited engagements for specific objectives.

Current Raid Types

Note: Conquest and Sabotage raids are currently Work in Progress and not yet available.

Plunder Raid (Active)

  • PvP enabled
  • No block destruction
  • Nexus invulnerable
  • Focus: Resource theft
  • Duration: 1 Hour

Conquest Raid (WIP)

  • Coming soon
  • Territory claims
  • No Nexus damage

Sabotage Raid (WIP)

  • Coming soon
  • Target infrastructure
  • Factories & weapons

Raid Mechanics (Active)

  • PvP Enabled: Combat is forced for the raid duration
  • No Block Destruction: Terrain and buildings are protected
  • Nexus Immunity: The defender's Nexus cannot be damaged
  • Time Limit: Plunder raids last 1 hour
Development Status: Conquest and Sabotage raids are under development. Only Plunder raids are currently available for limited resource-gathering operations.

5. Defense & Fortification

A well-defended Nexus is the key to surviving a siege. Defense requires a combination of strategic architecture, traps, and active player garrisoning—all while maintaining reasonable accessibility.

Principles of Nexus Defense

Layered Defense

Do not rely on a single wall. Create multiple defensive rings around your Nexus with chokepoints and kill zones. Each layer should slow attackers and give defenders opportunities to engage.

Active Garrison

A Nexus is only as strong as the players defending it. Establish a rotation of defenders, coordinate with your regiment leadership, and use voice communication for effective coordination.

Defensive Architecture

  • Obsidian Core: Reinforce the immediate Nexus chamber with obsidian for blast resistance
  • Murder Holes: Create openings above the Nexus chamber for defenders to fire down
  • Water Curtains: Strategic water flows can negate TNT damage and slow attackers
  • Trap Networks: Use redstone traps, arrow dispensers, and falling block traps

Formations for Defense

Regiments can spend RP on defensive formations that provide combat bonuses to all members in the area. These are activated through the Nexus GUI and can turn the tide of a siege.

Accessibility Reminder: While you are encouraged to build elaborate defenses, the Nexus must remain reachable. You cannot completely seal it off. Defensive structures must still allow a path—however dangerous—to the Nexus itself.

6. Diplomacy: Ultimatums & Peace

War is not the only option. GeoFrontier features a robust diplomatic system for resolving conflicts or making demands without a single arrow fired.

Ultimatums

An ultimatum is a formal demand issued to another nation or town before a war declaration. It allows you to state your terms and give your opponent a chance to comply. Issuing an ultimatum opens a negotiation GUI where you can propose various conditions.

Peace Treaties

Peace treaties are used to end an ongoing war. They utilize the same GUI as ultimatums, allowing both sides to negotiate terms for surrender or ceasefire.

/ultimatum issue <opponent>
Opens the negotiation GUI to issue a pre-war ultimatum with specific terms.
/peace propose <opponent>
Opens the negotiation GUI to propose terms for ending an active war.
/war peace
Requests an unconditional peace (surrender) with all parties you are currently at war with.

Negotiable Terms

Territory Transfer

Permanently transfer a town or chunk to the other party.

Occupation

Temporarily occupy a territory for up to 30 days. Control, but not ownership, is transferred.

Reparations

A one-time payment of in-game currency from one party to another.

Player Jailing

Specific players from the losing side can be jailed for up to 7 days.

Strategic Diplomacy: Use ultimatums to gauge an enemy's resolve. A strong, prepared nation might refuse, while a weaker one may concede. In peace negotiations, temporary occupation of a key resource zone can be a more achievable goal than demanding permanent cession.

1. The Nexus

The Nexus (an end crystal) is your town's heart. It has 1,000 HP and is vulnerable only during sieges. Its destruction dissolves your town. Use /nexus to manage wars, armies, and raids.

Placement Rules

  • Max depth: 15 blocks underground
  • Max height: 50 blocks above highest structure
  • Outside these bounds? Provide a clear path—defendable, but accessible.

Nexus Stats

  • HP: 1,000
  • Vulnerable: Only during sieges
  • Movable: /nexus move (peace only)

Placement

  • Max 15 down
  • Max 50 up
  • Path required if outside

Commands

/nexus
Opens Nexus GUI.
/nexus move
Relocates Nexus. Not during war.
/nexus remove
Deletes Nexus. Peace only.
/nexus create
Creates a new Nexus.
Staff relocate improperly placed Nexuses. You cannot move during war—plan ahead.

2. Army System & Regiment Points

To wage war, form an army. Each nation can have 3 army groups, each with 3 regiments.

Chain of Command

  • Nation leader creates army by joining a regiment (/army join).
  • Leader appoints a General per army group.
  • General may appoint Regiment Leaders for each of the 3 regiments.

Regiment Points (RP)

Kills during wars/raids earn RP for your regiment. Regiment Leaders spend RP in the Nexus GUI to purchase formations—temporary buffs for all regiment members.

Hierarchy

  • 3 Army Groups per nation
  • 3 Regiments per group
  • General + optional Regiment Leaders

Regiment Points

  • Earned from kills
  • Pooled by regiment
  • Spent on formations
Appoint Regiment Leaders so they can buy formations mid-battle without waiting on the General.

3. Siege Warfare

A formal war declaration triggers a siege—total conflict with no time limit. Ends when a Nexus is destroyed or a peace treaty is signed.

Siege Mechanics

  • PvP always on
  • Block destruction on
  • Nexus vulnerable (1,000 HP)
  • No time limit

Requirements to Declare War

  • Target set as enemy
  • At least one army group formed
  • Both sides at peace
  • Valid Nexuses
  • Nation leader or war perms

Commands

/war declare nation <nation>
Declare war on a nation.
/war declare town <town>
Declare war on a specific town.
/war status
Check your wars.
/war list
List all active wars.

Siege Features

  • PvP forced
  • Block breaking
  • Nexus damageable
  • No timer

Requirements

  • Enemy status
  • Army formed
  • Both at peace
  • Nexus placed

4. Raids

Raids are time-bound, objective-focused attacks. No block destruction. No Nexus damage.

General Raid Mechanics

  • PvP enabled
  • No block damage
  • Nexus invulnerable
  • Fixed duration

Raid Types

Plunder (Active)

1 hour. Steal resources.

Conquest (Soon)

Capture territory.

Sabotage (Soon)

Disable infrastructure.

Only Plunder raids are currently available.

5. War Rules

Violations result in penalties, including automatic war loss.

5.1 No Towering Over Walls

Go through walls, not over them. Penalty: Minor-Moderate.

5.2 No Lava/Obsidian-Filled Walls

Violating sections are set to air. Penalty: Minor-Moderate + contents cleared.

5.3 Alliance Declarations

Allies must be set before war. No mid-war betrayals/new allies. Penalty: 3x upkeep for a week for all involved.

5.4 Valid War Reasons

Sieges require a valid reason (raids/PvP exempt). Penalty: Automatic war loss.

5.5 No Instant-Kill Traps

No unavoidable death traps during wars. Penalty: Minor-Moderate + trap removal.

Staff enforce these rules. When in doubt, ask.

6. Fortification

Layered Defense

Multiple walls with kill zones. Don't rely on a single barrier.

Active Garrison

Defenders > obsidian. Coordinate with your regiment and use voice chat.

Defensive Architecture

  • Obsidian core: Blast-proof Nexus chamber.
  • Murder holes: Ceiling openings to fire down.
  • Water curtains: Mitigate TNT, slow attackers.
  • Trap networks: Arrow dispensers, falling blocks (no insta-kills).

Defensive Formations

Spend RP on defensive buffs via Nexus GUI.

You must leave a path—however dangerous—to the Nexus.

7. Diplomacy

Ultimatums

Issue pre-war demands via /ultimatum issue. If refused, you have a casus belli.

Peace Treaties

End wars with /peace propose (negotiated terms) or /war peace (unconditional surrender).

Negotiable Terms

Territory Transfer

Permanent town/chunk transfer.

Occupation

Temporary control (up to 30 days).

Reparations

One-time currency payment.

Player Jailing

Jail specific players (up to 7 days).

Ultimatums test enemy resolve. In peace talks, temporary occupation can be easier to secure than permanent cession.

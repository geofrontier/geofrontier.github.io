The Nexus

  • HP: 1,000 (vulnerable only during sieges)
  • Max depth: 15 blocks
  • Max height: 50 blocks above highest build
  • /nexus move — relocate (peacetime only)

Army Structure

  • 3 Army Groups per nation
  • 3 Regiments per group
  • Must be in a regiment to damage Nexus
  • Kills earn Regiment Points (RP)

Siege vs Raid

  • Siege: Block destruction, Nexus damage, no time limit
  • Raid: No block damage, Nexus safe, 1 hour limit
  • Must be enemies to declare war

Key Rules

  • No towering over walls
  • No lava-filled walls
  • No instant-kill traps
  • Alliances must be set pre-war

1. The Nexus System

The Nexus (an end crystal) is the heart of your town. It's the target in any conflict—if destroyed, your town is dissolved. Use /nexus to open the GUI for managing wars, armies, and raids.

Nexus Stats & Placement

  • Hit Points: 1,000 (only damageable during active sieges)
  • Vulnerability: Sieges only — completely invulnerable during peace
  • Movement: /nexus move to relocate (cannot be used during war)

Placement Rules (Staff Enforced)

The Nexus must be reasonably accessible to attackers. Staff will relocate violations.

  • Max depth: Cannot be buried more than 15 blocks underground
  • Max height: Cannot be more than 50 blocks above the highest structure or natural ground
  • Access path: If placed outside these bounds, you must provide a clear path (tunnel, bridge, etc.) that attackers can follow. This path can still be defended with walls and traps.

Nexus Commands

/nexus Open war management GUI
/nexus move Relocate Nexus (peace only)
/nexus create Create Nexus if missing
/nexus remove Permanently remove Nexus (peace only)
Critical: Staff will relocate improperly placed Nexuses. Since you can't move during war, ensure proper placement beforehand.

2. Army System

Critical rule: To damage an enemy Nexus during a siege, you must be a member of a regiment. Players not in a regiment cannot deal damage to Nexuses.

Army Hierarchy

  • 3 Army Groups maximum per nation
  • 3 Regiments maximum per army group (Regiments 1, 2, and 3)
  • General leads each army group
  • Regiment Leader leads each individual regiment

Joining a Regiment

Any nation member can join a regiment using:

/army join 1 Join Regiment 1
/army join 2 Join Regiment 2
/army join 3 Join Regiment 3

The first person to join a regiment (usually the nation leader) automatically creates that regiment and becomes its leader.

Appointing Leaders

Once regiments exist, leaders can be appointed through the Nexus GUI or commands:

  • Nation Leader can appoint Generals for each Army Group
  • Generals can appoint Regiment Leaders for the three regiments under them
/army leader set [player] 1 Set player as Regiment 1 leader
/army leader remove [player] Remove regiment leader status

Regiment Points (RP) & Formations

When a regiment member kills an enemy player during war or raids, their regiment earns Regiment Points (RP). These are pooled at the regiment level and can be spent by regiment leaders on formations—temporary buffs for all regiment members.

How Formations Work

1
Regiment earns RP from player kills during conflicts
2
Regiment leader opens Nexus GUI and selects a formation to purchase
3
Formation activates, granting combat buffs to all regiment members in the area
4
Buffs last for a limited duration — use strategically!

Leadership tip: Appoint regiment leaders so they can purchase formations in real-time during battle, without needing to contact the General. This creates a more responsive fighting force.

Remember: If you're not in a regiment, you cannot damage the enemy Nexus during sieges. Join a regiment before war is declared.

3. Siege Warfare

A formal war declaration escalates to a siege—total war with no time limit until one side's Nexus is destroyed or peace is signed.

Siege Mechanics

  • PvP: Forced between warring parties
  • Block destruction: Enabled — attackers can breach walls
  • Nexus: Vulnerable (1,000 HP becomes the target)
  • Nexus damage: Only regiment members can damage the Nexus
  • Duration: No time limit — continues until decisive conclusion

Requirements to Declare War

  • Target must be set as enemy diplomatically
  • Your nation must have at least one army group formed
  • Neither side can already be at war with each other
  • Both sides must have valid, placed Nexuses
  • You must be nation leader or have war permissions

War Commands

/war declare nation [nation] Declare war on entire nation
/war declare town [town] Declare war on specific town
/war status Check your war status
/war list List all active wars
/war peace Request unconditional surrender

4. Raids (Limited Operations)

Raids are time-bound operations with specific objectives. Unlike sieges, they don't allow block destruction or Nexus damage.

Raid Mechanics (All Types)

  • PvP: Enabled for duration
  • Block destruction: ❌ Disabled — buildings protected
  • Nexus: ❌ Cannot be damaged
  • Duration: Fixed time limit, then automatic end

Raid Types

Plunder Raid (Active)

Steal resources from enemy storage.

  • Duration: 1 hour
  • Objective: Resource extraction

Conquest Raid (Coming Soon)

Capture and hold territory.

  • Objective: Land claims shift

Sabotage Raid (Coming Soon)

Disable factories and infrastructure.

  • Objective: Industrial targets
Note: Only Plunder raids are currently available. Conquest and Sabotage are in development.

5. Rules of War

These rules are enforced during sieges. Violations result in penalties up to automatic war loss.

5.1 No Towering Over Walls

Attackers must breach walls, not build over them. Building towers or pillars to bypass fortifications is prohibited.

Penalty: Minor to moderate

5.2 No Lava-Filled Walls

Walls cannot be filled with lava or made entirely of obsidian in ways that make breaching impossibly tedious. Staff will remove offending wall sections.

Penalty: Section removed, minor to moderate

5.3 Alliances Must Be Pre-Declared

All alliances must be set before war begins. No mid-war alliance switching or sudden inclusions.

Penalty: 3x upkeep costs for up to a week for all involved

5.4 Valid War Reason Required

Full-scale sieges require a valid casus belli (justification). Raids and open-world PvP are exempt.

Penalty: Automatic war loss

5.5 No Instant-Kill Traps

Traps that instantly kill with no counterplay are prohibited. Defenses should create challenges, not unavoidable deaths.

Penalty: Trap removed, minor to moderate

When in doubt, ask staff. If you're unsure whether a tactic or defense is allowed, get clarification before implementing it.

6. Fortification Strategy

A well-defended Nexus combines layered architecture, active defenders, and smart use of game mechanics—while staying within placement rules.

Layered Defense

Create multiple defensive rings around your Nexus. Each layer should:

  • Slow attackers' advance
  • Force resource expenditure
  • Give defenders multiple firing positions

Active Garrison

No amount of obsidian replaces coordinated players. Use voice chat, establish rotations, and communicate with regiment leadership.

Defensive Architecture

  • Obsidian core: Reinforce the Nexus chamber itself
  • Murder holes: Openings above for defenders to fire down
  • Water curtains: Can negate TNT damage and slow movement
  • Trap networks: Non-instant-kill hazards to harass attackers

Defensive Formations

Use Regiment Points to purchase defensive formations that buff all regiment members in the area. Activate through the Nexus GUI during critical moments.

Accessibility reminder: You can build elaborate defenses, but the Nexus must remain reachable. There must be a path—however dangerous—to the Nexus itself.

7. Diplomacy: Ultimatums & Peace

Not all conflicts need to end in destruction. Use diplomacy to achieve goals without fighting—or to formalize victory.

Ultimatums

Before declaring war, issue an ultimatum through the Nexus GUI. State your terms and give the opponent a chance to comply. A refused ultimatum serves as a valid casus belli.

/ultimatum issue [opponent] Open negotiation GUI

Peace Treaties

Negotiate an end to active wars through the same GUI. Both sides can propose and agree on terms.

/peace propose [opponent] Open peace negotiation

Negotiable Terms

Territory Transfer

Permanent transfer of towns or chunks

Occupation

Temporary control (up to 30 days)

Reparations

One-time currency payment

Player Jailing

Jail specific players for up to 7 days

Diplomatic strategy: Use ultimatums to gauge enemy resolve. In peace talks, temporary occupation of key resources may be easier to achieve than permanent cession—and sets up future conflicts.

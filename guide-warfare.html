1 The Nexus System

Nexus War is the primary warfare system on GeoFrontier. Each town's Nexus represents its heart and soul - destroy it, and the town falls.

1,000 HP

Each Nexus has 1,000 hit points. When it reaches 0, the town loses the war.

Free on Creation

Every town receives a Nexus block automatically upon town creation.

Movable Nexus

Use /nexus move to relocate your Nexus (cannot move during war)

Visibility Rules

Nexus cannot be hidden underground (staff-enforced). Must be reasonably accessible.

/nexus Opens the Nexus GUI to manage wars and view status
/nexus move Moves your town's Nexus to your current location
/nexus remove Removes your town's Nexus (only during peace time)
/nexus create Creates a new Nexus for your town

Strategic Placement: Place your Nexus in a defensible but accessible location. Consider castle keeps, fortified bunkers, or central town squares with layered defenses. Use /nexus move to relocate if your current location is vulnerable.

Staff Enforcement: Hiding your Nexus underground, in bedrock, or in otherwise inaccessible locations is prohibited. Staff will move improperly placed Nexuses to surface locations. Nexus cannot be moved during active wars.

2 Starting Wars

Initiating warfare requires proper diplomatic status and strategic preparation.

/war declare nation (nation) Declares war on another nation
/war declare town (town) Declares war on a specific town
/war status Checks current war status and active conflicts
/war list Lists all active wars on the server

War Prerequisites:

  1. Your nation must have the target nation/town as an enemy (set through diplomacy)
  2. Both sides must be at peace (not already at war with each other)
  3. Your town must have a Nexus placed and accessible
  4. The target must have at least one town with a Nexus
  5. You must be the nation leader or have war permissions

Casus Belli: Consider roleplaying a reason for war (territorial dispute, resource conflict, historical grievance). This makes conflicts more engaging and can attract allies to your cause.

Example War Declarations:
To declare war on a nation: /war declare nation Carthage
To declare war on a specific town: /war declare town NewRome
Check active wars: /war list
Check your war status: /war status

3 Nexus Defense

Protecting your Nexus requires layered defenses, garrison troops, and strategic planning.

Defense Strategies:

  • Layered Defenses: Create multiple walls, moats, and kill zones around your Nexus
  • Garrison System: Station dedicated defenders in shifts during wartime
  • Trap Networks: Use redstone traps, lava moats, and arrow dispensers
  • Fallback Positions: Create inner sanctums with secondary defenses
  • Scouting Network: Place observers to detect approaching enemy forces

Defensive Architecture:

  • Use obsidian for critical defensive structures (expensive but resilient)
  • Create murder holes and arrow slits for defensive firing positions
  • Build underground escape tunnels for defenders (but Nexus must remain accessible!)
  • Use water to slow attackers and prevent TNT damage
  • Place mounted weapons (cannons, arrow towers) around your Nexus

Defense Limits: While you can fortify heavily, the Nexus chamber must remain reasonably accessible. Completely sealing the Nexus in bedrock or making it physically impossible to reach is against the rules. Staff will enforce proper placement.

4 Raid Types

Raids are limited military actions available through the Nexus GUI. They allow targeted attacks without full-scale war.

Plunder Raid

Attack towns and plunder their resources (not annex or occupy)

Duration: 1 hour

Conquest Raid

Occupy territory from towns (not full town annexation)

Duration: 2 hours

Sabotage Raid

Sabotage infrastructure like mounted weapons, factories, etc.

Duration: 30 minutes

Raid Strategy:

  • Plunder Raids: Quick resource grabs. Target wealthy but poorly defended towns
  • Conquest Raids: Strategic territory acquisition. Target border towns or resource-rich areas
  • Sabotage Raids: Weaken enemies before full war. Target factories, defenses, infrastructure
  • All raids are initiated through the Nexus GUI (open with /nexus)

Raid Limitations:

  • Raids have time limits (1h, 2h, 30m)
  • Cannot destroy Nexus during raids
  • Raids don't trigger full war status
  • Defenders can fight back during raid duration
  • Raids can be used against both nations and individual towns

5 Peace & Ultimatums

Negotiate the end of conflicts or make demands before war through sophisticated diplomacy systems.

/peace propose (opponent) Proposes a peace treaty to end a war. Opens GUI for setting terms
/ultimatum issue (opponent) Issues an ultimatum with demands before declaring war. Opens GUI for setting terms
/war peace Requests unconditional peace with all warring parties

Peace Treaty Negotiation Interface. See below for GUI features

Territory Transfer

Transfer towns or territory

Up to 30 days

Territory Occupation

Occupy towns or territory without transfer

Up to 30 days

Financial Terms

Send or receive money as reparations or tribute

One-time payment

Player Jailing

Jail specific players as part of peace terms

Up to 7 days

Peace Treaty vs Ultimatum:

  • Peace Treaty: Used to end existing wars. Negotiate terms after conflict has begun.
  • Ultimatum: Used to make demands before declaring war. "Do this or we will attack."
  • Both: Use the same GUI interface with the same available terms.
  • Negotiation: Terms can be negotiated back and forth until both parties agree. Such as transferring different regions in return for X.

Diplomatic Strategy:

  • Ultimatums: Use to make demands without fighting. Can be a bluff or serious threat.
  • Peace Treaties: Negotiate from strength when winning, from weakness when losing.
  • Financial Terms: Demand reparations from aggressors or offer tribute to stronger enemies.
  • Territory: Consider temporary occupation vs permanent transfer based on your goals.
  • Player Jailing: Use to remove key enemy leaders or troublemakers from future conflicts.

Complete War Example:
1. Scout enemy: Identify Nexus location and defenses
2. Send Ultimatum: /ultimatum issue Carthage - demand resources
3. If refused: /war declare nation Carthage
4. Launch Sabotage Raid: Weaken factories first (30m)
5. Full assault: Attack Nexus with combined forces
6. If winning: /peace propose Carthage with favorable terms
7. If losing: Request /war peace to minimize losses

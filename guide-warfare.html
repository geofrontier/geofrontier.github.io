1 The Nexus System

Nexus War is the primary warfare system on GeoFrontier. Each town's Nexus represents its heart and soul - destroy it, and the town falls.

1,000 HP

Each Nexus has 1,000 hit points. When it reaches 0, the town loses the war.

Free on Creation

Every town receives a Nexus block automatically upon town creation.

War Condition

The Nexus cannot be moved or hidden during wartime - it must be defended!

Visibility Rules

Nexus cannot be hidden underground (staff-enforced). Must be reasonably accessible.

/nexus Opens the Nexus GUI to manage wars and view status
/nexus remove Removes your town's Nexus (only during peace time)
/nexus create Creates a new Nexus for your town

Strategic Placement: Place your Nexus in a defensible but accessible location. Consider castle keeps, fortified bunkers, or central town squares with layered defenses.

Staff Enforcement: Hiding your Nexus underground, in bedrock, or in otherwise inaccessible locations is prohibited. Staff will move improperly placed Nexuses to surface locations.

2 Starting a War

Initiating warfare requires proper diplomatic status and strategic preparation.

/n enemy add (nation) Declares another nation as an enemy (prerequisite for war)
/nexus declarewar (nation) Declares war on a nation (opens GUI confirmation)

War Prerequisites:

  1. Your nation must have the target nation set as an enemy using /n enemy add (nation)
  2. Both nations must be at peace (not already at war with each other)
  3. Your town must have a Nexus placed and accessible
  4. The target nation must have at least one town with a Nexus
  5. You must be the nation leader or have war permissions

Casus Belli: Consider roleplaying a reason for war (territorial dispute, resource conflict, historical grievance). This makes conflicts more engaging and can attract allies to your cause.

Example War Declaration:
1. First, set the nation as enemy: /n enemy add Carthage
2. Open Nexus GUI: /nexus
3. Select "Declare War" from the GUI
4. Choose "Carthage" from the list of enemy nations
5. Confirm the declaration

3 Nexus Defense

Protecting your Nexus requires layered defenses, garrison troops, and strategic planning.

/nexus status Checks your Nexus's current HP and defense status
/nexus reinforcements Shows reinforcement status of Nexus defenses

Defense Strategies:

  • Layered Defenses: Create multiple walls, moats, and kill zones around your Nexus
  • Garrison System: Station dedicated defenders in shifts during wartime
  • Trap Networks: Use redstone traps, lava moats, and arrow dispensers
  • Fallback Positions: Create inner sanctums with secondary defenses
  • Scouting Network: Place observers to detect approaching enemy forces

Defensive Architecture:

  • Use obsidian for critical defensive structures (expensive but resilient)
  • Create murder holes and arrow slits for defensive firing positions
  • Build underground escape tunnels for defenders (but Nexus must remain accessible!)
  • Use water to slow attackers and prevent TNT damage

Defense Limits: While you can fortify heavily, the Nexus chamber must remain reasonably accessible. Completely sealing the Nexus in bedrock or making it physically impossible to reach is against the rules.

4 Attacking Enemy Nexuses

Successful siege warfare requires coordination, intelligence, and overwhelming force.

Phase 1: Reconnaissance

Scout enemy territory before declaring war. Identify Nexus location, defensive weaknesses, and approach routes. Consider using spies or aerial reconnaissance.

Phase 2: Siege

Breach outer defenses using TNT, cannons, or tunneling. Coordinate multiple attack vectors to divide defender attention. Use artillery barrages to soften defenses.

Phase 3: Assault

Storm the inner defenses with melee troops while archers provide covering fire. Use shields, potions, and coordinated pushes. Target the Nexus directly once accessible.

Phase 4: Nexus Destruction

Focus all firepower on the Nexus. It has 1,000 HP and can be damaged by any weapon. Protect your own assault troops while dealing the final blows.

Assault Tactics:

  • Use cannons from Movecraft ships for naval bombardment of coastal towns
  • Create distraction attacks on other fronts to draw defenders away
  • Time your assault for when enemy defenders are offline (but respect timezone fairness)
  • Bring obsidian breakers (efficiency V diamond picks) for breaching strong defenses

5 War Conclusion & Aftermath

Wars end when one side's Nexus is destroyed or peace terms are negotiated.

/nexus peace (nation) /nexus surrender

Post-War Strategy:

  • Rebuild damaged defenses immediately after war
  • Consider relocating your Nexus if its location was compromised
  • Strengthen alliances to prevent future attacks
  • Analyze what went wrong (or right) to improve for next time

War Rules:

  • No griefing beyond what's necessary for Nexus assault
  • Respect offline players - don't attack when zero defenders are online
  • No exploiting Nexus placement rules
  • Honor peace treaties and truce periods
  • Report any rule violations to staff immediately

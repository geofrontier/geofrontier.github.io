NavyCraft

Age of Exploration Naval System — Build, Sail, and Conquer

Work in Progress

NavyCraft is currently being developed and balanced. Ship stats and mechanics may change. This guide will be updated as the system evolves.

Naval System Overview

NavyCraft replaces the standard Movecraft system with specialized Age of Exploration ship classes. Each ship type has specific building requirements, capabilities, and combat roles. The seas are extremely dangerous — only properly built ships will survive storms, creatures, and enemy fire.

Ship System Notes

Fuel Efficiency: Ships vary in fuel consumption. Smaller ships like Caravel and Corsair are very fuel-efficient, while larger ships consume more resources.

Cruise Skip: Determines how many blocks the ship "skips" during cruising mode. Higher values mean smoother sailing over long distances.

Durability: All ships can take damage and sink. Man-o-War has the highest durability (90% sink threshold).

Tech Restrictions: All ships forbid redstone, pistons, and other modern technology to maintain era authenticity.

Forbidden Blocks (Tech Restrictions)

The following blocks are prohibited on all NavyCraft ships: Pistons, Sticky Pistons, Slime Blocks, Honey Blocks, Observers, Dispensers, Droppers, Redstone components, Beacons, and Ender Chests. These restrictions maintain Age of Exploration authenticity and prevent exploits.

Fuel System

All ships require fuel to sail. Fuel efficiency varies by ship class:

Fuel Types & Values
Coal: 15-50 units
Charcoal: 15-50 units
Coal Block: 150-500 units
Lava Bucket: 75-1000 units
Efficiency Examples
Most Efficient: Caravel, Corsair
High Efficiency: Frigate, Man-o-War
Moderate: Schooner, Brigantine
Lowest: Galleon

Note: Fuel consumption is balanced so larger warships aren't prohibitively expensive to operate, while still requiring resource investment.

Ship Progression Path

Start (Coastal): Pinnace (20-150) or Cutter (151-400) for learning and quick travel

Early Exploration: Caravel (600-2500) for discovery voyages and light trade

Specialization: Schooner (500-1400) for versatility or Brigantine (1200-2800) for combat

Mid-Game Power: Corsair (2500-4400) for raiding or Frigate (4200-6500) for naval power

Late-Game Trade: Galleon (2501-4000) for bulk cargo and treasure transport

End-Game Flagship: Man-o-War (7500-10000) as ultimate naval dominance

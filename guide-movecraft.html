Age of Exploration Ships

Movecraft allows you to build and pilot historically-inspired watercraft from the Age of Exploration era. Each ship type has specific size limits, speeds, and combat capabilities.

Sloop

Fast, small vessel ideal for scouting and quick raids. Max 500 blocks.

Brigantine

Medium warship with good speed and firepower. Max 1,200 blocks.

Frigate

Powerful warship with heavy cannons. Max 2,500 blocks.

Galleon

Massive trading vessel with immense cargo space. Max 4,000 blocks.

Historical Accuracy: While we encourage historically-inspired designs, minor creative liberties are allowed. Stick to wooden construction with sail propulsion for the authentic Age of Exploration feel.

1 Ship Types & Limits

Each ship classification has specific block limits and characteristics designed for different roles in exploration and naval warfare.

/craft list Lists all available ship types and their limits

Ship Classifications:

  • Sloop: 100-500 blocks, speed 8, ideal for solo players
  • Schooner: 300-800 blocks, speed 7, good balance of speed and firepower
  • Brigantine: 600-1,200 blocks, speed 6, standard warship
  • Frigate: 1,000-2,500 blocks, speed 5, heavy warship
  • Galleon: 2,000-4,000 blocks, speed 4, trading/command ship

Block Limits: Exceeding the maximum block count will prevent your ship from being piloted. Use /craft info while looking at your ship to check block counts.

Ship Composition: Your ship must be at least 70% wood-based blocks (oak, spruce, birch, etc.). Stone and iron may be used for reinforcement but should be minimal. Sails should be made from wool or white concrete.

2 Building Your Ship

Constructing a seaworthy vessel requires careful planning and attention to essential components.

/craft pilot Assumes pilot control of the ship you're standing on
/craft release Releases control of the ship

Essential Components:

  • Helm Block: Place a sign with "[CraftName]" on the first line (e.g., "[Frigate]")
  • Movement Blocks: At least 80% of blocks must be contiguous (touching)
  • Waterline: Ship must float naturally in water - no flying ships
  • Cannon Ports: Leave openings in the hull for cannons (2-block minimum width)
  • Sails: Required for movement. Use wool, carpet, or white concrete

Building Tip: Start with a skeleton frame using fences or walls, then fill in the hull. Ensure your ship has adequate buoyancy - wider hulls are more stable but slower.

Example Helm Sign:
Line 1: [Frigate]
Line 2: HMS Victory (Ship name - optional)
Line 3: YourNation (Nation tag - optional)
Line 4: (Leave blank)

3 Piloting Commands

Once your ship is built and you're at the helm, use these commands to navigate the seas.

/craft start Activates the ship's engines (requires pilot permission)
/craft stop Stops the ship immediately
/craft cruise Toggles cruise control for steady sailing
/craft ascend
/craft descend Adjusts ship depth (for submarines or avoiding shallows)

Movement Controls:

  • Forward/Backward: Look in the direction you want to go
  • Turning: Look left/right while moving forward
  • Speed: Determined by ship type and wind direction
  • Momentum: Larger ships take longer to stop and turn

Navigation Tip: Use map items to chart your course. Plot trade routes between coastal towns and avoid known pirate waters unless you're prepared for combat.

Collision Warning: Ships take damage from collisions with land, other ships, and underwater obstacles. Monitor your speed near coastlines!

4 Naval Combat

Engage enemy vessels with historically accurate naval warfare mechanics.

/craft fire Fires all loaded cannons on your ship
/craft reload Reloads all cannons (requires gunpowder and cannonballs)

Combat Mechanics:

  • Broadside Attacks: Position your ship sideways to fire maximum cannons
  • Aiming: Cannons fire in the direction they're facing on your ship
  • Damage: Hits destroy blocks and create holes in enemy hulls
  • Sinking: Ships sink when water fills critical areas below the waterline
  • Boarding: Ramming allows players to board enemy vessels for melee combat

Combat Strategy:

  • Keep your powder magazine (gunpowder storage) deep in the ship's interior
  • Angle your approach to present a smaller target
  • Target enemy sails to reduce their mobility
  • Use islands and fog for ambushes

Cannon Placement: Cannons must be placed on wool blocks facing outward through 2-block wide ports. Each cannon requires 1 iron block, 1 dispenser, and 2 signs marked "[Cannon]" on the first line.

5 Repair & Maintenance

Keep your vessel seaworthy with regular maintenance and battle damage repair.

/craft repair Initiates repair mode (requires materials in inventory)
/craft info Shows ship integrity, damage, and block counts

Maintenance Requirements:

  • Daily Upkeep: Ships deteriorate if not used regularly
  • Damage Repair: Replace destroyed blocks with matching materials
  • Hull Integrity: Ships sink if too many blocks below waterline are destroyed
  • Sail Damage: Torn sails reduce speed by up to 50%

Dry Docks: Build a sheltered bay or dry dock area in your nation's territory for safe repairs. Use pistons or water gates to create an enclosed repair area.

Abandoned Ships: Ships left unattended for over 7 days may be removed by staff. Use /craft dock at a recognized nation dock to register your ship as stored.

6 Rules & Restrictions

Follow these rules to ensure fair and enjoyable naval gameplay for everyone.

/craft help Shows all Movecraft commands and basic usage

Server Rules for Naval Play:

  • No Airships: All vessels must be watercraft that float naturally
  • Historical Theme: Designs should reflect Age of Exploration era ships (1400-1800)
  • Nation Registration: All warships must be registered to a nation
  • No Griefing: Attacking undefended civilian ships is prohibited
  • Port Respect: No combat within 100 blocks of neutral ports
  • Size Limits: Respect the block limits for each ship class

Good Seamanship:

  • Yield to smaller vessels in narrow channels
  • Keep lights visible at night (use sea lanterns or glowstone)
  • Sound your horn (note blocks) when approaching blind corners
  • Assist sinking vessels when not at war

Enforcement: Violations of naval rules may result in ship confiscation, fines, or temporary bans from using Movecraft. Repeated offenses lead to permanent naval privileges revocation.

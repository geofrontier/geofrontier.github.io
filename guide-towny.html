1 Creating a Town

Before you can establish a town, you need to find suitable land. Walk to an area that is not already claimed by another town—you can check this by looking at the chat messages as you move between chunks, or by using the /towny map command to see nearby claims. The land should be at least four chunks away from any existing town's homeblock (the chunk containing their town spawn).

Once you've found an unclaimed location, ensure you have enough money. Town creation costs $200, which you can check with /balance. If you have the funds, simply run:

/town new YourTownName
Creates your town at the exact chunk you're standing in. The chunk becomes your "homeblock"—the heart of your town where your spawn point will be.

After successful creation, you're now the mayor. Your town starts with 8 claim blocks (enough for an 8-chunk territory) and the ability to invite other players. You'll want to immediately set your town spawn using /town set spawn at a safe location, and consider setting an initial town board message with /town set board [message] to communicate with future residents.

New Town Checklist

  1. Set your spawn — /town set spawn at a safe, accessible location
  2. Invite residents — /town invite PlayerName to grow your town
  3. Claim surrounding chunks — /town claim to expand your borders
  4. Set town permissions — Decide who can build (see Section 5)
  5. Consider joining a nation — Nations provide claim bonuses (see Section 7)
Strategic tip: Choose a location near resources (wood, stone, water) but defensible. Flat terrain is easier to build on, while mountains offer natural protection. Consider proximity to other towns for future alliances.

2 Claiming Land

Territory in Towny is claimed in chunks—16×16 block areas. Each chunk claimed costs money, and the price increases with each new claim. You claim land to secure resources, control access to your town, and expand your influence.

Basic Claiming

To claim the chunk you're standing in, simply use /town claim. The cost will be deducted from your town bank (deposit money with /town deposit [amount]). For larger expansions, /town claim auto [number] claims multiple adjacent chunks outward from your existing territory.

How Claiming Costs Work

The first chunk you claim costs $5.00. Each subsequent claim costs 2% more than the previous one, up to a maximum of $175 per chunk. This means early expansion is cheap, but claiming very large territories becomes expensive. If you later unclaim land, you receive a 15% refund of the original cost.

Building Standards

Standard building: Minimum footprint of 8×8 blocks (half a chunk) and must be fully enclosed (walls + roof).
Large building: Minimum footprint of 16×16 blocks (1 full chunk). Should be visually prominent—whether a tall tower or a sprawling manor—clearly distinguishable from ordinary houses.
Specialty building:
  • Occupies at least 8 chunks in total (contiguous or integrated design).
  • Serves a civic, military, economic, industrial, or maritime function.
  • Visually distinct and purpose‑built (e.g. port, armoury, university).
  • Factories and Capital buildings are excluded from counting as specialty buildings (factories are required separately; capitals are for M5).
  • Each specialty building may only be counted once (no duplicates).

Milestone Claims – Staff‑reviewed progression

Towns may request a milestone review in Discord. Staff evaluate sequentially from M1 upward. You must complete all previous milestones before qualifying for the next. The requirements now focus on accumulating specialty buildings (distinct, large‑scale constructions) as your town develops.

Review process: Open a Discord support ticket to request a milestone evaluation. Staff will check M1, then M2, etc., and award all completed milestones. You cannot skip a milestone – each step’s prerequisites must be satisfied.

Distance Rules

  • Homeblock protection: Your town's homeblock (spawn chunk) is protected by a 4-chunk buffer—no other town can claim within 4 chunks of it.
  • Adjacent claims: Towns can claim directly next to each other's regular plots (0 chunk minimum).
  • Same nation exception: Towns within the same nation ignore these distance restrictions, allowing them to claim right next to each other.
Outposts disabled: Traditional outpost creation (claiming disconnected chunks) is not available. Use the Embassy System (Section 11) for remote teleportation.

3 Managing Residents

A town grows through its residents. As mayor, you can invite players, assign ranks to delegate responsibilities, and remove troublesome members.

Inviting and Removing Residents

To invite a player, use /town invite [player]. The player must accept the invitation using /town accept. To remove a resident, use /town kick [player]. Note that assistants cannot be kicked—they must be demoted first.

Available Ranks

Ranks grant special permissions to trusted residents. Assign them with /town rank add [player] [rank]:

co-mayor 2nd in command. Shares full powers with the mayor except deletion of town.
assistant Management powers: can invite/kick residents, claim/unclaim land, and set permissions.
sheriff Law enforcement: can jail players within town and manage outlaws.
helper Can assist with permissions: allowed to set permissions on town and individual plots.

Mayoral Succession

If a mayor becomes inactive for 30 days without announcing any notices or permenantly banned, a co-mayor can open a ticket and request to be transferred ownership.

Resident Limits

  • Maximum residents per town: Unlimited
  • Maximum plots per resident: Unlimited
  • Minimum time before joining a town: None—new players can be invited immediately

4 Taxes & Upkeep

Every town must pay daily upkeep to the server. If your town bank runs out of money, the town enters bankruptcy and will be deleted after 7 days. You can fund your town through resident taxes.

Setting Taxes

/town set tax [amount]
Sets daily tax per resident (max $1,000 flat or 15% percentage)
/town set plottax [amount]
Sets tax for players who own individual plots (max $1,000)
/town deposit [amount]
Adds personal money to the town bank
/town withdraw [amount]
Removes money from town bank (use carefully)

Upkeep Costs

Towns pay $2.00 per claimed chunk each day. For example, a town with 50 claimed chunks pays $100 daily. Nations also pay upkeep: $1,000 per day for the nation itself (not per town).

If your town has claimed more land than your resident count allows (overclaiming), you pay an additional $100 per extra chunk per day.

Bankruptcy

When a town cannot pay its upkeep, it goes into debt. If the debt reaches the town's value cap and remains unpaid for 7 days, the town will be automatically deleted. Keep your town bank funded!

Warning: Towns that cannot pay upkeep enter bankruptcy. After 7 days at the debt cap, the town is deleted and all land becomes unclaimed.
Strategy: Balance taxes to cover upkeep without driving residents away. Use /town to check your daily costs and current balance.

5 Town Permissions

Permissions control who can build, destroy, use switches, and fight within your town. You can set them for four groups: residents (town members), nation (members of your nation), ally (allied nations), and outsider (everyone else).

Basic Permission Command

/town set perm [group] [type] on/off
Sets permission for a group. Types: build, destroy, switch, itemuse, pvp

Common Permission Configurations

  • /town set perm outsider build off — Prevent outsiders from building anywhere in town
  • /town set perm resident pvp off — Disable PvP between town members
  • /town set perm ally switch on — Allow allies to use doors, buttons, levers
  • /town set perm nation build on — Let all nation members build (useful for shared projects)

Outlaw System

You can designate players as outlaws using /town outlaw add [player]. Outlaws:

  • Can be attacked anywhere in your town, regardless of PvP settings
  • Cannot enter your town—they will be teleported away after 5 seconds
  • Cannot use Ender Pearls or Chorus Fruit while in your town
  • If killed in your town, they are automatically jailed for 2 hours

Plot-Specific Permissions

Individual plots can override town-wide permissions using /plot set perm [group] [type] on/off.

Plot owners pay daily taxes based on the town's plot tax setting. Embassy plots have a default 10% tax rate.

7 Joining a Nation

Nations are alliances of towns that provide significant benefits: collective defense and shared resources. Towns with 5 or more residents can form their own nation; towns with up to 14 residents can remain independent, but joining a nation is highly recommended for growth.

Creating or Joining a Nation

To create a nation, your town must have at least 5 residents and $10,000 in the town bank. Stand in your town and run:

/nation new [Name]
Creates a nation with your town as the capital
/nation join [Nation]
Request to join an existing nation
/nation leave
Leave your current nation (if you're not the capital)

Nation Benefits

  • Shared protection: Towns in the same nation can claim directly adjacent (ignoring distance rules)
  • Collective defense: Attackers killed in any town are jailed for the entire nation
  • Nation chat: Use /nc [message] to communicate with all nation members
  • Nation spawn: Teleport to the capital with /nation spawn
  • Upkeep discounts: Larger nations receive a discount on nation upkeep (not town upkeep)

9 Death & PvP Economics

When you die in Player-vs-Player combat, you lose money to your killer. Only PvP deaths cost money—dying to monsters, falls, or environmental causes has no financial penalty. When killed by another player, you lose 5% of your personal balance, up to a maximum of $1,000 per death. The lost money is paid directly to your killer.

Risk management: High-wealth players risk significant losses even with the $1,000 cap. Consider keeping some wealth in town banks rather than your personal balance.

10 Jail System

Towns with jail plots can imprison outlaws and enemy combatants. If you have a jail plot, enemies and outlaws who die in your town are automatically imprisoned: Outlaws: 2 hours, Enemy combatants: 5 hours.

Jail Costs and Management

  • Setup fee: $1,000 one-time cost
  • Hourly cost: $250 per prisoner
  • Maximum sentence: 12 hours

Bail System

Standard prisoner$1,000
Captured mayor$5,000
Captured king$10,000

Important: Jailed players can still use Ender Pearls and Chorus Fruit to teleport. Build your jail cells with this in mind.

11 Embassy System

Since direct teleportation to towns outside your nation is disabled, embassies are the only way to establish fast travel between different nations. An embassy is a special plot that, once approved by staff, allows your town to teleport directly to that location.

How to Establish an Embassy

The process requires cooperation between two towns and staff approval:

1Travel to the target town — Physically journey to the town you want to connect with.
2Request an embassy plot — The receiving town sets it with /plot set type embassy (costs $5,000).
3Build your embassy — Construct a government-style building, take screenshots.
4Apply in Discord — Submit your embassy build for approval.
5Staff approval — Once approved, teleport using /town outpost.

Embassy Teleportation Rules

  • Cooldown: 1 hour
  • Cost: $500 per teleport
  • Tax: 10% default
  • Usage: Only members of the town that built the embassy can teleport to it
Critical: Without an approved embassy, you CANNOT teleport to towns outside your nation.

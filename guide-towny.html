1 Creating a Town

Establishing your first settlement is the foundation of your journey on GeoFrontier. A town provides protection, organization, and a home for your citizens.

/town new <TownName> Creates a new town with your current location as the town center (Cost: $500)

Tip: Choose a strategic location near resources but defensible. Consider proximity to water, mountains for natural defenses, and resource availability.

Requirement: You need $500 and must be in an unclaimed area. New towns must be at least 4 chunks from any existing town's homeblock. Check available funds with /balance.

2 Claiming Land

Expand your town's territory to secure resources and build defensive perimeters. Towny uses a chunk-based system (16×16 blocks).

/town claim Claims the chunk you're standing in for your town
/town claim auto <number> Claims multiple adjacent chunks at once
/town deposit <amount> Adds money to your town bank for claiming costs

Example: To claim 5 chunks at once with $50 each, first deposit $250: /town deposit 250 then /town claim auto 5

Claiming Costs & Mechanics

  • Base Claim Cost: $5.00 per townblock
  • Increasing Costs: Each additional claim costs 5% more than the last (cumulative)
  • Bonus Blocks: Can purchase extra claim blocks for personal use at $100.00 each (increases by 20% each purchase)
  • Claim Limits: Towns can claim 10 townblocks per resident

Growth Strategy: As claiming costs increase with each plot, the most effective way to expand is by recruiting more residents. Each new member allows 8 more claims at the base price!

Claim Distance Rules

  • Homeblock Buffer: No other town can claim within 4 chunks of your town's homeblock
  • Town Spacing: New towns must be at least 4 chunks from any existing town's homeblock
  • No Plot Buffer: Towns can claim directly adjacent to each other's plots (0 chunk minimum)

Important: Outposts have no distance restrictions from other towns and no maximum distance from your main town. Place them strategically!

3 Managing Residents

A thriving town needs citizens. Invite players, assign ranks, and build your community.

/town invite <PlayerName> Invites a player to join your town
/town kick <PlayerName> Removes a player from your town
/town rank add <PlayerName> <rank> Assigns a special rank to a resident

Available Ranks:

  • assistant - Can invite/kick residents, claim/unclaim land
  • helper - Can set permissions on town/plots
  • jailer - Can jail players within town
  • taxexempt - Exempt from town taxes

4 Taxes & Upkeep

Manage your town's economy to ensure long-term survival and growth.

/town set tax <amount> Sets daily tax collected from each resident
/town set plottax <amount> Sets tax for individual plot owners
/town withdraw <amount> Withdraws money from town bank to yourself

Upkeep Calculation

  • Base Upkeep: $40.00 daily
  • Plot-Based: Upkeep is calculated as (number of claimed plots × $40.00)
  • Overclaim Penalty: $100.00 per day for each plot over your limit
  • Overclaim Calculation: Penalty is (plots over limit × $100.00)

Important: If your town bank cannot pay the daily upkeep, your town will enter "bankrupt" status. After 7 days bankrupt, chunks will be unclaimed automatically!

Strategy: Balance taxes to cover upkeep without driving residents away. Use /town to check your town's daily costs and current balance.

5 Town Permissions

Control what players can do within your town's borders for security and organization.

/town set perm <who> <type> <on/off> Sets town-wide permissions for different groups

Permission Groups:

  • resident - Town members
  • ally - Members of allied nations
  • outsider - Everyone else
  • nation - Members of your nation

Permission Types:

  • build - Place blocks
  • destroy - Break blocks
  • switch - Use buttons, levers, etc.
  • itemuse - Use items (buckets, flint & steel)
  • pvp - Player vs player combat

Common Setup:
/town set perm outsider build off - Prevents outsiders from building
/town set perm resident pvp off - Prevents PvP between residents
/town set perm ally switch on - Allows allies to use switches

6 Plot Management

Divide your town into specialized districts and manage individual land plots.

/plot fs <price> Puts your current plot up for sale
/plot claim Claims a plot that's for sale (stand in it)
/plot set type <type> Sets the plot type (affects permissions)
/plot set perm <who> <type> <on/off> Sets permissions for this specific plot

Plot Types & Specializations

  • default - Standard town plot
  • shop [C] - Commercial area (blue on map)
  • arena [A] - PvP enabled combat zone
  • embassy [E] - Diplomatic zone ($1,000 to create)
  • inn [I] - Hospitality area
  • jail [J] - Prison facility
  • farm [F] - Agricultural zone

Strategy: Create commercial districts with shop plots near town spawn, residential areas with build permissions for residents only, and embassy plots near borders for diplomatic relations.

7 Joining a Nation

Forge alliances and gain protection by forming or joining a nation with other towns.

/nation new <NationName> Creates a new nation with your town as capital ($10,000 cost)
/nation join <NationName> Requests to join an existing nation
/nation ally <NationName> Proposes an alliance with another nation

Nation Features

  • Neutrality: Nations can declare neutrality for $1,000 (excludes you from wars)
  • Town Merging: Towns can merge for free base cost + 50% of claim cost per plot
  • Shared Protection: Allied towns within same nation ignore claim distance restrictions
  • Collective Defense: Attackers jailed in any town's jail are imprisoned for the nation

Nation Benefits:

  • Shared protection and military support
  • Reduced upkeep costs for towns
  • Ability to declare war and conduct diplomacy
  • Access to nation-only chat channels
  • Collective bargaining power in server politics

8 Useful Commands

Essential commands for daily town management and navigation.

/town Opens town management interface
/resident Shows your resident information
/town spawn Teleports to your town spawn point
/town set spawn Sets town spawn to your location
/town here Shows information about current town
/town list Lists all towns on the server
/town toggle fire Toggles fire spread in your town
/town toggle explosion Toggles explosion damage in your town

Pro Tip: Use /town ? to see all available town commands. Many commands have tab-completion - press TAB to see options!

9 Death & PvP Economics

Understand the financial consequences of death and player combat on GeoFrontier.

Death Costs

  • PvP Deaths Only: Death costs only apply when killed by another player
  • Percentage Loss: Lose 5% of your personal balance when killed in PvP
  • Maximum Loss: Capped at $1,000.00 per death (even if 5% would be higher)
  • Payment: The amount lost is paid to your killer

Example Death Costs:
• $10,000 balance → Lose $500 (paid to killer)
• $50,000 balance → Lose $1,000 (capped, paid to killer)
• $5,000 balance → Lose $250 (paid to killer)
• Environmental deaths (fall, mobs) → No cost

Combat Strategy: Consider your bank balance before engaging in PvP. High-wealth players risk significant losses even with the $1,000 cap!

10 Jail & Justice System

Towns can establish jails to imprison enemies and outlaws who attack their territory.

Automatic Imprisonment

  • Outlaws: Players with outlaw status killed in your town are automatically jailed for 1 hour
  • Jail Requirements: Town must have a plot set as "jail" type

Jail Management

  • Jail Costs: $1,000 initial fee + $250 per hour per prisoner (paid from town bank)
  • Maximum Sentence: Mayors can jail for up to 12 hours
  • Escape Prevention: Jailed players cannot use /town leave
  • Teleport Items: Jailed players CAN use Ender Pearls and Chorus Fruit!

Security Warning: Build your jail cells secure! Prisoners can use teleport items, so consider obsidian boxes, lava barriers, or other creative containment methods.

Bail System

  • Standard Bail: $1,000 for regular prisoners
  • Mayor Bail: $10,000 for captured town mayors
  • King Bail: $25,000 for captured nation kings
  • Max Bail: Mayors can set bail up to $1,000 for regular crimes

Economic Warfare: Capturing enemy leaders can be highly profitable through bail payments, but requires secure holding facilities!

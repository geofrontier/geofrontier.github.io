1 Creating a Town

Establishing your first settlement is the foundation of your journey on GeoFrontier. A town provides protection, organization, and a home for your citizens. To create a town, you must be in an unclaimed area at least 4 chunks from any existing town's homeblock.

Requirements

  • Cost: $500 (check balance with /balance)
  • Location: Unclaimed territory, minimum 4 chunks from other towns
  • Command: /town new <TownName> creates town at your current location
Strategic tip: Choose a location near resources but defensible. Consider proximity to water, mountains for natural defenses, and resource availability.
Requirement: You need $500 and must be in an unclaimed area. New towns must be at least 4 chunks from any existing town's homeblock.

2 Claiming Land

Expand your town's territory to secure resources and build defensive perimeters. Towny uses a chunk-based system (16×16 blocks).

Claim Commands

/town claim
Claims the chunk you're standing in for your town
/town claim auto <number>
Claims multiple adjacent chunks at once
/town deposit <amount>
Adds money to your town bank for claiming costs

Claiming Costs

ItemValue
Base Claim Cost$5.00 per townblock
Cost ProgressionEach claim costs 5% more than the last (cumulative)
Bonus Blocks$100.00 each (increases 20% per purchase)
Claim Limit10 townblocks per resident
Growth strategy: As claiming costs increase with each plot, the most effective way to expand is by recruiting more residents. Each new member allows 8 more claims at the base price!

Claim Distance Rules

  • Homeblock Buffer: No other town can claim within 4 chunks of your town's homeblock
  • Town Spacing: New towns must be at least 4 chunks from any existing town's homeblock
  • No Plot Buffer: Towns can claim directly adjacent to each other's plots (0 chunk minimum)
Outposts: Have no distance restrictions from other towns and no maximum distance from your main town. Place them strategically!

3 Managing Residents

A thriving town needs citizens. Invite players, assign ranks, and build your community.

Resident Commands

/town invite <Player>
Invites a player to join your town
/town kick <Player>
Removes a player from your town
/town rank add <Player> <rank>
Assigns a special rank to a resident

Available Ranks

assistant Invite/kick residents, claim/unclaim land
helper Set permissions on town/plots
jailer Jail players within town
taxexempt Exempt from town taxes

4 Taxes & Upkeep

Manage your town's economy to ensure long-term survival and growth.

Tax Commands

/town set tax <amount>
Sets daily tax collected from each resident
/town set plottax <amount>
Sets tax for individual plot owners
/town withdraw <amount>
Withdraws money from town bank to yourself

Upkeep Calculation

Base Upkeep$25.00 daily
Plot-BasedNumber of claimed plots × $40.00
Overclaim Penalty$100.00 per day per plot over limit
Overclaim Formula(plots over limit × $100.00)
Bankruptcy: If your town bank cannot pay daily upkeep, your town enters "bankrupt" status. After 7 days bankrupt, chunks will be unclaimed automatically.
Strategy: Balance taxes to cover upkeep without driving residents away. Use /town to check your town's daily costs and current balance.

5 Town Permissions

Control what players can do within your town's borders for security and organization.

Command: /town set perm <who> <type> <on/off> — Sets town-wide permissions for different groups.

Permission Groups

  • resident — Town members
  • ally — Members of allied nations
  • outsider — Everyone else
  • nation — Members of your nation

Permission Types

  • build — Place blocks
  • destroy — Break blocks
  • switch — Use buttons, levers, doors
  • itemuse — Use items (buckets, flint & steel)
  • pvp — Player vs player combat

Common configurations

  • /town set perm outsider build off — Prevents outsiders from building
  • /town set perm resident pvp off — Prevents PvP between residents
  • /town set perm ally switch on — Allows allies to use switches

7 Joining a Nation

Forge alliances and gain protection by forming or joining a nation with other towns.

Nation Commands

/nation new <Name>
Creates nation with your town as capital ($10,000)
/nation join <Nation>
Requests to join an existing nation
/nation ally <Nation>
Proposes alliance with another nation

Nation Features

  • Neutrality: Declare neutrality for $1,000 (excludes you from wars)
  • Town Merging: Free base cost + 50% of claim cost per plot
  • Shared Protection: Allied towns ignore claim distance restrictions
  • Collective Defense: Attackers jailed in any town are imprisoned for the nation

Nation Benefits

  • Shared protection and military support
  • Reduced upkeep costs for towns
  • Ability to declare war and conduct diplomacy
  • Access to nation-only chat channels
  • Collective bargaining power

9 Death & PvP Economics

Understand the financial consequences of death and player combat.

Death Costs (PvP only)

Loss Percentage5% of personal balance when killed by player
Maximum LossCapped at $1,000 per death
PaymentLost amount paid to your killer
Environmental DeathsNo cost (falls, mobs, etc.)

Examples

  • $10,000 balance → Lose $500 (paid to killer)
  • $50,000 balance → Lose $1,000 (capped, paid to killer)
  • $5,000 balance → Lose $250 (paid to killer)
Combat strategy: Consider your bank balance before engaging in PvP. High-wealth players risk significant losses even with the $1,000 cap!

10 Jail & Justice System

Towns can establish jails to imprison enemies and outlaws who attack their territory.

Automatic Imprisonment

  • Outlaws: Players with outlaw status killed in your town are automatically jailed for 1 hour
  • Jail Requirements: Town must have a plot set as "jail" type

Jail Management

Initial Fee$1,000
Hourly Cost$250 per prisoner (paid from town bank)
Maximum Sentence12 hours (set by mayors)
Escape PreventionJailed players cannot use /town leave
Teleport ItemsEnder Pearls and Chorus Fruit can be used
Security: Build your jail cells secure! Prisoners can use teleport items, so consider obsidian boxes, lava barriers, or other creative containment methods.

Bail System

Standard Bail$1,000
Mayor Bail$10,000
King Bail$25,000
Maximum Bail Set$1,000 for regular crimes
Economic warfare: Capturing enemy leaders can be highly profitable through bail payments, but requires secure holding facilities!

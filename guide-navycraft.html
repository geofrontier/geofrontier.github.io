Work in Progress

NavyCraft is currently being developed and balanced. Ship stats and mechanics may change. This guide will be updated as the system evolves.

Naval System Overview

NavyCraft replaces the standard Movecraft system with specialized Age of Exploration ship classes. Each ship type has specific building requirements, capabilities, and combat roles. The seas are extremely dangerous — only properly built ships will survive storms, creatures, and enemy fire.

Ship Classes

Choose your vessel based on your needs: exploration, trade, or warfare. Each class has size limits, speed, and weapon capacity.

Critical: Storm Resistance

Wooden ships will be destroyed in storms! For ocean-going vessels, reinforce critical areas with Iron Blocks, Stone Bricks, or Obsidian. See the Dangerous Seas Guide for survival requirements.

Ship Progression Path

Start: Pinnace or Caravel for learning and coastal travel

Early Game: Merchantman for trade or Corsair for raiding

Mid Game: Frigate for protection or specialized vessels (Xebec, Fluyt)

Late Game: Galleon or Manowar for naval power

End Game: First-rate as ultimate flagship

NavyCraft Commands

Basic Controls

/craft sail - Start sailing

/craft release - Stop sailing

/craft info - Ship status

Combat

/craft fire - Fire cannons

/craft reload - Reload weapons

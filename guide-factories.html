1. How Factories & Extraction Sites Work

Think of these as automated production lines for your town. Once built and approved, they run on their own—you just collect the results.

Extraction Sites (Passive)

These sites generate resources automatically over time. You build them, turn them on with /extract toggle, and resources appear in the chest at regular intervals. No input needed.

Factories (Active)

Factories consume materials to produce specific items. You put ingredients in the chest, run /factory produce [item], and after a short time the finished product appears. You must supply the materials each time.

Key difference: Extraction sites = passive income (free resources over time). Factories = conversion (turn raw materials into valuable items).

Critical Rule (Read This)

All sites require staff approval before they'll work. You cannot just place a chest and run commands. You must:

  1. Build a proper structure (minimum 6 chunks total volume, looks like a factory/mine/farm)
  2. Apply in Discord with screenshots
  3. Wait for staff to approve and activate it
  4. Then use commands on the chest

Warning: If you build a site without applying first, it will be removed. Always get approval before building.

2. Step-by-Step: From Idea to Production

Follow these steps exactly. If you skip any, your site won't work.

Complete Walkthrough

1
Choose your site type — Decide whether you want an extraction site (passive resources) or a factory (item crafting). Check the costs in Quick Start to ensure you have enough money in your town bank.
2
Find a location — Pick a spot in your town. Remember: extraction sites work best in appropriate biomes (farms on plains, quarries in mountains, logging camps in forests).
3
Build the structure — Construct a building that meets the requirements:
  • Minimum 6 chunks total volume (example: 3 chunks long × 2 chunks wide × 1 chunk high = 6 chunks)
  • Must look like what it is — factories need smokestacks, mechanical details; mines need supports, scaffolding; farms need silos, irrigation
  • Place a chest inside where items will go
4
Take screenshots — Capture exterior and interior shots showing the building and the chest. Good lighting helps.
5
Apply in Discord — Open a ticket in #support with title "Factory Application" or "Extraction Site Application". Include:
  • Your town name and coordinates
  • What type of site (quarry, armory, etc.)
  • Screenshots of the build
6
Wait for staff review — Staff will visit your town to inspect. If approved, they'll activate the site. You'll get a confirmation in Discord.
7
Create the site in-game — Stand in front of the chest and run the appropriate command:
  • Extraction: /extract create [type] (cost deducted from town bank)
  • Factory: /factory create [type]
8
Start production — For extraction sites: /extract toggle. For factories: put materials in chest, then /factory produce [item].
9
Collect your resources — Items will appear in the chest automatically. Check back regularly.

Pro tip: Label your chest with a sign (e.g., "Quarry Output" or "Armory Input") so you remember which chest is which if you have multiple sites.

Extraction sites produce resources automatically once toggled on. You don't need to feed them materials—just turn them on and collect.

Commands for Extraction Sites

Important: All commands must be typed while looking directly at the chest.

/extract create quarry Create quarry ($10k)
/extract create logging_camp Create logging camp ($8k)
/extract create farm Create farm ($5k)
/extract create mining_outpost Create mining outpost ($15k)
/extract toggle Start or stop production

Tip: Extraction sites work while you're offline. Log on later to find a chest full of resources.

Example: Making a Musket

1
Build armory (6+ chunks, looks like weapons workshop)
2
Apply in Discord, get approved
3
Stand at chest, type /factory create armory ($5k deducted)
4
Put 5 Iron, 2 Stick, 1 Flint in chest
5
Type /factory produce musket while looking at chest
6
Wait 60 seconds — musket appears in chest

Note: Siege Works and Wagon Works are currently disabled server-wide. Don't build them yet.

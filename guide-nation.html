Why Form a Nation?

A nation is more than just a collection of towns—it's a sovereign entity with the power to wage war, form alliances, and shape server politics. Nations provide military protection, economic benefits, and diplomatic influence.

Military Protection

Collective defense, shared armies, and coordinated warfare against rival nations

Diplomatic Power

Form alliances, negotiate treaties, and participate in server-wide politics

Economic Advantages

Reduced upkeep costs, nation-wide taxes, and shared economic resources

Community & Influence

Greater visibility on the server, voting power in global decisions

Prerequisite: You must be the mayor or co-mayor of an established town before creating a nation. Ensure your town is stable with sufficient funds and claimed territory.

1 Creating a Nation

Establish your nation's sovereignty and claim your place on the geopolitical stage. Your town becomes the capital, and you become the nation's leader.

/nation new <NationName> Creates a new nation with your town as the capital

Naming Strategy: Choose a name that reflects your nation's identity. Consider historical, geographical, or ideological themes that will attract like-minded towns.

Requirements:

  • Must be mayor or co-mayor of a town
  • Requires $5,000 in your town bank
  • Your town must have at least 5 claimed chunks
  • Nation name must be unique and 3-20 characters

Example: If your town is named "Carthage" and you want to create a Mediterranean-themed nation:
/nation new CarthaginianRepublic

2 Managing Members

Expand your nation's influence by inviting other towns to join. A strong nation is built on trust and shared goals.

/nation add <TownName> Invites a town to join your nation
/nation kick <TownName> Removes a town from your nation
/nation set capital <TownName> Changes the nation's capital to another member town

Member Management Strategies:

  • Vetting Process: Screen potential members for activity, alignment with your nation's goals, and geographical proximity
  • Geographic Cohesion: Towns close together are easier to defend and coordinate
  • Specialization: Invite towns that complement your nation's needs (mining towns, agricultural centers, trading hubs)
  • Loyalty: Build trust through shared projects and regular communication

Pro Tip: Use /nation list to see all nations and their members. Research potential allies or rivals before making diplomatic moves.

3 Diplomacy & Alliances

Master the art of diplomacy to secure your nation's future through alliances, treaties, and strategic relationships.

/nation ally <NationName> Proposes an alliance with another nation
/nation enemy <NationName> Declares another nation as an enemy
/nation neutral <NationName> Sets neutral relations with a nation

Diplomatic States:

  • Ally: Full military cooperation, shared intelligence, economic bonuses
  • Neutral: Non-aggression, limited interaction
  • Enemy: Hostile relations, war possible

Diplomatic Strategy: Balance your alliances carefully. Too many allies can drag you into unwanted conflicts, while too few leave you vulnerable. Consider creating a "web of alliances" that deters aggression.

Warning: Breaking an alliance has serious consequences. You'll suffer a diplomatic reputation penalty, making it harder to form future alliances. Consider all outcomes before making diplomatic changes.

4 Warfare & Defense

When diplomacy fails, a nation must be prepared for war. Master the mechanics of conflict to defend your lands and expand your territory.

/nation declarewar <NationName> Declares war on another nation
/nation peace <NationName> Proposes peace to end a war
/nation set spawn Sets the nation spawn point (king's location)

War Mechanics:

  • War Declaration: 24-hour notice period before hostilities begin
  • War Goals: Capture enemy towns, destroy infrastructure, force surrender
  • Peace Terms: Negotiate reparations, territorial concessions, or alliances
  • Truce Period: 7-day forced peace after war ends

Defensive Strategy: Fortify border towns, establish fallback positions, and coordinate with allies. Use natural terrain (mountains, rivers) as defensive barriers.

Offensive Strategy:

  • Scout enemy territory before declaring war
  • Target economic centers to cripple their finances
  • Use diversionary attacks to split enemy forces
  • Coordinate timing with your allies

5 Economy & Taxes

A strong economy fuels military campaigns, infrastructure projects, and nation growth. Master financial management to ensure your nation's prosperity.

/nation set tax <amount> Sets the tax rate for member towns
/nation deposit <amount> Deposits money into the nation bank
/nation withdraw <amount> Withdraws money from the nation bank

Economic Strategy:

  • Taxation Balance: Set taxes high enough to fund nation projects but low enough to avoid member rebellion
  • Infrastructure Investment: Use nation funds to build roads, defenses, and public facilities
  • War Chest: Maintain a reserve fund for emergency military expenses
  • Economic Specialization: Encourage member towns to specialize in different economic sectors

Bankruptcy Risk: If your nation cannot pay its weekly upkeep (based on number of towns and chunks), member towns will automatically leave. Monitor your nation's finances with /nation regularly.

6 Government & Ranks

Establish a government structure to manage your nation effectively. Assign roles, set permissions, and create a chain of command.

/nation rank add <player> <rank> Assigns a special rank to a player
/nation set title <title> Sets your nation's official title (Kingdom, Republic, etc.)

Available Nation Ranks:

  • King/Queen: Nation leader, all permissions
  • Chancellor: Can manage finances, diplomacy, and member towns
  • General: Can declare war, manage military operations
  • Senator: Can vote on nation decisions (if democracy enabled)
  • Ambassador: Can negotiate with other nations

Government Types:

  • Monarchy: Single ruler with absolute power
  • Republic: Elected officials, voting system
  • Oligarchy: Rule by council of elites
  • Federation: Member towns retain autonomy with shared defense

7 Territory & Expansion

Control strategic territory to secure resources, establish defensive borders, and project power across the server.

/nation claim Claims land for your nation (king only)
/nation unclaim Unclaims nation land
/nation outpost <TownName> Creates a military outpost in a town

Territorial Strategy:

  • Natural Borders: Use mountains, rivers, and oceans as natural defenses
  • Resource Control: Secure areas with rare resources (diamonds, emeralds, special ores)
  • Strategic Chokepoints: Control mountain passes, narrow land bridges, and river crossings
  • Buffer Zones: Maintain neutral territory between you and potential enemies

Overextension Risk: Claiming too much territory spreads your defenses thin and increases upkeep costs. Focus on quality (strategic locations) over quantity.

8 Advanced Commands

Master these advanced commands to optimize your nation's management and gain an edge over your rivals.

/nation rename <newName> Renames your nation (costs $10,000)
/nation merge <NationName> Proposes a merger with another nation
/nation set tag <tag> Sets your nation's tag (3-4 characters)
/nation stats Shows detailed nation statistics
/nation board <message> Sets the nation's message board
/nation list online Lists all nations with online members

Pro Tips:

  • Use /nation ? to see all available commands
  • Regularly check /nation stats to monitor growth
  • Set a clear message board with /nation board to communicate with members
  • Use nation tags for quick identification in chat and on maps

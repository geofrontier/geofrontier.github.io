1 Staff Roles & Positions

Our staff team is structured to provide effective community management while offering growth opportunities for dedicated members.

Moderator (Mod)

Entry-level staff position. Handles rule enforcement, player assistance, and basic server monitoring.

Lead Moderator

Experienced mod who mentors new staff, handles complex situations, and assists with team coordination.

Administrator (Admin)

Senior staff with technical permissions, plugin configuration access, and higher-level decision making.

Developer

Technical role focused on plugin development, server optimization, and custom feature creation.

Career Progression: Most staff members start as Moderators and can progress to higher positions based on performance, dedication, and demonstrated skills. Be honest about where you're looking to end up within our community in your application.

General Staff Responsibilities:

  • Rule Enforcement: Fairly and consistently apply server rules
  • Player Assistance: Help players with questions, issues, and disputes
  • Community Building: Foster a positive, welcoming environment for all players
  • Conflict Resolution: Mediate disputes between players or nations
  • Server Monitoring: Keep an eye on chat, player behavior, and server performance
  • Event Support: Help organize and run server events

2 What We Look For

Successful staff applicants share certain qualities and characteristics that make them effective community leaders.

Availability

Consistent, reliable hours that match our community's peak times

Communication

Clear, respectful communication with players and staff

Fairness

Ability to apply rules consistently without bias

Passion

Genuine interest in helping our community thrive

Application Tips:

  • Be specific: Instead of "I like helping people," explain how you've helped players in the past
  • Highlight relevant experience: Both Minecraft and non-Minecraft moderation experience is valuable
  • Show your personality: We want to know who you are, not just what you can do
  • Be honest about availability: It's better to under-promise and over-deliver
  • Explain your motivations: Why do you want to be staff on GeoFrontier specifically?

Common Application Mistakes:

  • One-word answers: Provide detailed responses that show thought and effort
  • Exaggerating experience: Be honest about what you have and haven't done
  • Focusing only on power: We're looking for helpers, not rulers
  • Poor formatting: Use paragraphs and proper grammar in your responses
  • Not following instructions: Answer all questions completely

3 Application Process

Understand what happens after you submit your application and what to expect from our review process.

Step 1: Application

Submit your completed application through the form below

Step 2: Review

Our lead mods and admins will review your application

Step 3: Voice Call

If accepted, we'll schedule a quick voice call to get to know you

Step 4: Onboarding

Successful applicants will be shown around the team and trained

Voice Call Preparation: If your application is accepted, we'll schedule a quick voice call (VC) to get to know you better. This is a casual conversation, not an interrogation. Be prepared to talk about your experience, your interest in the server, and your availability. This helps us ensure you're a good fit for our team culture.

What Happens After Submission:

  1. Application Review: Our lead mods and admins will review your application
  2. Decision: You will receive a decision via Discord (the platform you provide)
  3. Voice Call: If accepted, we'll schedule a quick voice call at a mutually convenient time
  4. Onboarding: You'll be introduced to the team, given permissions, and trained on procedures
  5. Trial Period: Most new staff begin with a trial period to ensure good fit

Strong Application Responses:
Question: "What interests you about applying?"
Weak Answer: "I want to be staff."
Strong Answer: "I've been playing on GeoFrontier for 3 months and love the community. I particularly enjoy helping new players understand the factory system and would like to contribute more formally to maintaining the positive environment here."

4 Application Form

Use the form below to submit your staff application. Please fill out all fields completely and thoughtfully.

Form Preparation Tips:

  • Prepare answers offline: Write your responses in a separate document first, then paste them into the form
  • Check your usernames: Ensure your Discord and Minecraft usernames are exact and current
  • Be specific about availability: Include timezone and specific hours you're typically available
  • Think about career goals: Consider where you'd like to end up (Mod, Lead Mod, Admin, etc.)
  • Show personality: The "tell us about yourself" question is your chance to stand out
  • Proofread: Check for spelling and grammar errors before submitting

5 Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about the staff application process and their answers.

How long does the application process take?

After applying, one of our lead mods or admins will reach out to you with a decision. The timeline varies based on application volume and staff availability, but we try to review applications within a week.

Do I need previous moderation experience?

While previous experience (within or outside Minecraft) is helpful and should be mentioned in your application, it's not strictly required. We value attitude, availability, and community knowledge just as highly.

What if I don't know which position to apply for?

Most applicants start by applying for Moderator positions. If you have specific technical skills (development, building, etc.), mention them in your application and we can discuss specialized roles during the voice call.

Is there a minimum age requirement?

While we don't have a strict age requirement, we expect all staff to demonstrate maturity, good judgment, and professional communication regardless of age.

How much time do I need to commit?

This varies by position. Moderators typically commit 5-10 hours per week, while higher positions may require more. Be honest about your availability in your application.

What happens during the voice call?

The voice call is a casual conversation to get to know you better, discuss your application, answer your questions, and see if you're a good fit for our team culture. It typically lasts 15-30 minutes.

Can I reapply if I'm not accepted?

Yes, but we recommend waiting at least 30 days before reapplying. Use the time to become more active in the community and address any feedback you may have received.
